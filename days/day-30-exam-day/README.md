## During the Exam (115 minutes)

### Time Management Strategy

**First 60 minutes: Questions 1-35**
- Maintain steady pace (~1.7 min/question)
- Answer confidently
- Flag difficult questions
- Don't get stuck

**Next 45 minutes: Questions 36-65**
- Continue steady pace
- Stay focused
- Flag if unsure
- Keep moving forward

**Last 10 minutes: Review**
- Review flagged questions
- Double-check answers
- Trust your instincts
- Submit with confidence

### Question-Answering Strategy

**For Each Question:**

1. **Read Carefully**
   - Read the entire question
   - Watch for "NOT", "EXCEPT", "LEAST"
   - Identify what's being asked

2. **Eliminate Wrong Answers**
   - Cross out obviously incorrect options
   - Narrow down to 2 choices
   - Consider edge cases

3. **Choose Best Answer**
   - Select most appropriate option
   - Consider scenario requirements
   - Think about best practices

4. **Flag if Unsure**
   - Mark for review
   - Make your best guess
   - Move on quickly

5. **Don't Overthink**
   - Trust your preparation
   - First instinct often correct
   - Don't read too much into it

### Stay Calm and Focused

**If you feel anxious:**
- Take a deep breath
- Close your eyes for 5 seconds
- Remember your preparation
- Refocus on the question

**If you're stuck:**
- Flag the question
- Move on
- Come back later
- Don't waste time

**If you're running out of time:**
- Answer all remaining questions
- Quick educated guesses
- No penalty for wrong answers
- Submit before time expires

## Exam Tips & Tricks

### Watch Out For

**"NOT" Questions:**
- "Which is NOT true..."
- "Which does NOT apply..."
- Read extra carefully!

**Absolute Words:**
- "always", "never", "must", "cannot"
- Usually indicate wrong answers
- Snowflake has many exceptions

**"All of the Above":**
- Verify EACH option is correct
- If one is wrong, eliminate this choice
- Don't assume all are correct

**Similar Options:**
- Look for subtle differences
- Consider specific scenarios
- Think about limitations

### Trust Your Preparation

- You've studied for 30 days
- You've taken 2 practice exams
- You've scored well on quizzes
- You know this material
- Trust yourself!
